What is PRP treatment?

PRP (Platelet-Rich Plasma) therapy uses a concentrate prepared from a small sample of your own blood. The platelet-rich portion contains growth factors that are applied or injected to support the skin's natural renewal. At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, PRP is physician-led and prepared under sterile, medical conditions.

We use systems such as Cellenis PRP for facial rejuvenation and for scalp treatment as part of a hair-restoration plan. Because PRP is derived from your own blood, it is a regenerative approach tailored to you.

PRP Facial Hair Restoration Cellenis PRP Skin Rejuvenation Under-Eye Acne Scars

How it works

  1. Consultation. A CANVAS physician assesses your skin or scalp and confirms whether PRP is appropriate for you.
  2. Blood draw. A small sample of your blood is taken, similar to a routine test.
  3. Preparation. The sample is processed to concentrate the platelet-rich plasma under sterile conditions.
  4. Application. The PRP is injected or applied with microneedling to the target area, then aftercare is reviewed by your physician.

FAQ

PRP treatment, answered.

PRP is prepared from a small sample of your own blood. It is processed to concentrate the platelet-rich plasma, which contains growth factors that support natural skin and tissue renewal.
Because PRP uses your own blood, the risk of an allergic reaction is low. At CANVAS it is prepared under sterile conditions and administered by a physician after screening to confirm suitability.
PRP is usually delivered as a series — often a few sessions spaced several weeks apart, with maintenance thereafter. Your physician will plan this with you.
You may notice mild redness or minor swelling for a short period, especially when PRP is combined with microneedling. Most patients resume normal activities quickly.
