What is Cryocord Exosomes?

Cryocord is Malaysia's leading stem cell bank — the same partner behind CANVAS's stem cell therapy programme — and its exosome preparations are derived from ethically donated, laboratory-processed umbilical cord tissue. Cord-derived exosomes are prized in regenerative medicine for the youthfulness of their signalling profile: renewal instructions from the youngest tissue there is.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Cryocord exosomes are used within physician-supervised skin renewal and recovery protocols — typically delivered via microneedling or after laser and RF. Provenance is the point: banked, certified, laboratory-grade biology rather than product of uncertain origin, in a category where sourcing is everything.

How it works

  1. Consultation & programme design. Your physician places Cryocord exosomes within your renewal or recovery plan — or advises honestly if simpler options serve first.
  2. Preparation. Microneedling or your scheduled energy treatment opens the delivery pathway.
  3. Exosome application. The cord-tissue-derived preparation is applied across the treated area.
  4. Course & review. Two to four sessions typical, reviewed against your skin's response.

Exosome therapy at CANVAS is a physician-supervised aesthetic treatment, not a treatment for medical disease. Not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active infection. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Why provenance decides everything in exosomes

Exosome products differ enormously in source tissue, processing standards and verification — and the category's promise attracts products with none of the three. Cryocord's preparations come from Malaysia's premier stem cell bank: donated cord tissue, laboratory processing, certified cold chain. That is the standard we consider non-negotiable before an emerging therapy touches patient skin.

Cryocord exosomes sit alongside Exoshine on the formulary and within our wider stem cell therapy partnership — one regenerative ecosystem, physician-supervised end to end.
