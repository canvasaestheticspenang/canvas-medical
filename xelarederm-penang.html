What is Xelarederm?

Xelarederm is built on the 'redermalisation' concept: pairing hyaluronic acid with succinic acid, a molecule central to cellular energy metabolism (the Krebs cycle) with recognised antioxidant activity. The HA rehydrates the dermis; the succinic acid pushes tired cells back toward energetic, defended function — hydration plus activation.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Xelarederm suits skin dulled by oxidative wear — sun, city, stress — where tone has gone grey-ish and elasticity slack. The face, neck, décolletage and hands all respond, making it a favourite for evening out the ages of exposed zones.

HA + succinic acid Redermalisation Cellular energy & antioxidant Tone & elasticity Face · Neck · Décolletage · Hands Three-session standard course

How it works

  1. Consultation & assessment. Your physician confirms oxidative dullness and tone are the target, and maps zones.
  2. Treatment session. The HA-succinic complex is microinjected evenly across the area, numbing as needed.
  3. Activation phase. Hydration lands early; energy and antioxidant support renew tone over the following weeks.
  4. Course & maintenance. Three sessions two to three weeks apart, then top-ups every four to six months.

Xelarederm is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active skin infection. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Succinic acid: the energising partner

Succinic acid sits at the centre of how cells make energy, and oxidative stress — sun chief among its causes in Malaysia — is precisely what drains skin's energetic and antioxidant reserves. Pairing it with HA turns a booster into a renewal treatment: hydration plus a push back toward youthful cellular function.

Sister formulations Electri and Inbiotec Amber work the same axis; your CANVAS physician selects within the succinic family by skin and zone.
