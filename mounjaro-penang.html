What is Mounjaro?

Mounjaro is the brand name for tirzepatide, a once-weekly injectable that acts on two gut-hormone receptors at once — GIP and GLP-1. By mimicking both, it reduces appetite, slows gastric emptying and improves the body's response to food, helping patients eat less and feel full sooner. In clinical trials it has produced among the greatest average weight reductions of any medical weight-loss treatment to date.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Mounjaro is offered strictly as prescription medicine within a supervised programme. It is not a shortcut sold over the counter: our physicians assess your suitability, prescribe and titrate the dose gradually to manage side effects, and review you throughout. It works best paired with the nutrition and activity guidance we build into every plan.

How it works

  1. Medical assessment. A physician reviews your health history, medications, weight goals and — where indicated — blood work, confirming whether tirzepatide is appropriate and safe for you.
  2. Prescription & titration start. If suitable, treatment begins at a low dose and steps up gradually over weeks, a schedule designed to minimise side effects.
  3. Weekly self-injection. The medicine is a once-weekly subcutaneous injection you can administer yourself after instruction, on a consistent day each week.
  4. Scheduled reviews. Your physician monitors weight, tolerance and dose throughout titration and maintenance — adjusting the plan to your response.

Who it's for

Mounjaro may be suitable if you:

  • Have struggled to lose weight through diet and exercise alone
  • Meet the medical eligibility criteria confirmed at assessment
  • Want a once-weekly option with strong clinical evidence
  • Are willing to be supervised and reviewed rather than self-medicating
  • Understand it is a medical treatment with possible side effects
  • Want weight management integrated with body contouring or wellness care

Mounjaro is a prescription medicine with eligibility criteria and possible side effects; it is not suitable for everyone, and not during pregnancy or breastfeeding. It is a medical treatment, not a cosmetic quick-fix, and works best alongside nutrition and activity. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Mounjaro vs Wegovy vs Rybelsus

Mounjaro (tirzepatide) acts on two receptors (GIP and GLP-1) and has shown the largest average weight loss in trials. Wegovy (semaglutide) is a once-weekly GLP-1 injectable with a large, well-established evidence base. Rybelsus is daily oral semaglutide in tablet form — the needle-free option.

None is universally 'best' — the right choice depends on your health profile, goals, tolerance and preferences. Your CANVAS physician will explain the options honestly and recommend accordingly at consultation.
