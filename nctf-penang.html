What is NCTF Vials?

NCTF is French laboratory FILLMED's signature — arguably the most famous formula in mesotherapy. Each vial carries hyaluronic acid alongside a matrix of some sixty ingredients: vitamins, amino acids, minerals, coenzymes and nucleic-acid components, composed to resupply everything a working dermis consumes.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, NCTF is the maximalist of the booster formulary — chosen for run-down, lacklustre skin that wants everything topped up at once, with a particular reputation for freshening the under-eye area and restoring all-over vitality.

~60-ingredient complex HA + vitamins + aminos + minerals Glow & vitality Under-eye freshening French mesotherapy icon Three-session course

How it works

  1. Consultation & review. Your physician confirms broad resupply suits your skin — and maps zones including under-eye if indicated.
  2. Treatment session. NCTF is microinjected superficially across the area; numbing keeps it comfortable.
  3. Resupply takes hold. The nutrient matrix supports dermal function; glow and vitality build week on week.
  4. Course & maintenance. Three sessions two to three weeks apart, then top-ups every three to six months.

Who it's for

NCTF Vials may be suitable if you:

  • Have run-down, lacklustre skin needing broad resupply
  • Look tired around the eyes despite sleeping well
  • Want the reference polyrevitalisation formula
  • Are restoring vitality after stress, illness or neglect
  • Prefer classics with long global track records
  • Rotate boosters and want the comprehensive leg

NCTF is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active skin infection. Vitamin sensitivities are screened at consultation. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

NCTF vs targeted boosters

Specialist boosters bet on one mechanism — concentration (Luhilo), amino supply (Jalupro), succinic energy (Xelarederm). NCTF bets on breadth: resupply the whole pantry and let the skin take what it needs.

Run-down skin with no single dominant deficit is NCTF territory; a clear dominant problem deserves its specialist. That diagnosis is your CANVAS physician's first job.
