What is Sculptra?

Sculptra is an injectable made of poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA), a biocompatible and biodegradable material used in medicine for decades. It is not a conventional filler: instead of adding volume directly, the PLLA microparticles act as a scaffold that stimulates your own fibroblasts to produce fresh collagen where the skin has thinned and hollowed with age.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Sculptra is used for diffuse, natural-looking rejuvenation — softly restoring temples, cheeks and jawline definition without the abrupt, 'done' look a heavy filler can produce. Because the result is built from your own collagen, it develops gradually and reads as you, simply more rested.

How it works

  1. Consultation & facial mapping. Your physician assesses volume loss, skin quality and facial proportions, and plans how many vials and sessions your goals realistically need.
  2. Treatment session. Reconstituted Sculptra is placed into the deeper skin layers across the planned zones. The session is comfortable, with numbing available.
  3. Collagen builds — weeks 2 to 8. The initial water volume settles within days; your own collagen then builds gradually where the PLLA was placed.
  4. Course completion & review. Most programmes involve two to three sessions, four to six weeks apart, with results reviewed at each visit and refined as they develop.

Who it's for

Sculptra may be suitable if you:

  • Notice hollowing at the temples, cheeks or along the jawline
  • Prefer a gradual, natural change over an instant filler effect
  • Want to address early facial deflation before it advances
  • Have had fillers before and want something that builds your own tissue
  • Are comfortable with results that develop over a course of sessions
  • Want a longer-lasting foundation that other treatments can build on

Sculptra is not used in the lips or tear troughs, and is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or with active infection in the treatment area. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Sculptra vs dermal fillers

A hyaluronic acid filler adds volume the moment it is injected — precise, immediate and ideal for defined structural work. Sculptra works on a different axis: it triggers your own collagen over weeks, producing a softer, more diffuse restoration that suits broad areas of age-related deflation.

The two are not rivals. At CANVAS, Sculptra frequently forms the regenerative foundation of a plan, with targeted dermal fillers used sparingly afterwards for definition where it matters. Your physician will design the sequence at consultation.
