What is Laser treatment?

Laser treatment uses precisely calibrated light energy to address pigmentation, uneven tone, texture and unwanted hair. At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, every laser session is selected and supervised by our physicians so the device, wavelength and settings suit your individual skin.

Our laser suite at Gurney Walk includes the PicoStar pico laser for pigment and tone, and the Dermablate cool laser for resurfacing. Treatments are tailored to your concern rather than applied off-the-shelf.

PicoStar Pico Laser Dermablate Resurfacing Pigmentation Laser Toning Laser Hair Removal Skin Texture

How it works

  1. Consultation & skin assessment. A CANVAS physician examines your skin, reviews your history and confirms whether laser is appropriate for you.
  2. Device & settings selection. The wavelength, energy and pulse are chosen for your concern and skin tone — pico laser for pigment, resurfacing for texture.
  3. Treatment. The handpiece delivers controlled light energy to the target area. Most patients describe a warm, tingling sensation; numbing cream is used where needed.
  4. Aftercare & review. We guide you on sun protection and recovery, and plan any follow-up sessions for gradual, natural results.

Who it's for

Laser treatment suits a wide range of concerns, but suitability is always confirmed at consultation. You may be a good candidate if you are looking to:

  • Address pigmentation, sunspots or an uneven complexion
  • Improve skin texture, enlarged-looking pores or acne scarring
  • Brighten dull skin and restore a more even tone
  • Reduce unwanted hair with a longer-term solution
  • Begin a maintenance plan for healthier-looking skin

FAQ

Laser treatment, answered.

Laser can be suitable for many skin tones, but the right device and settings matter. At your consultation a CANVAS physician assesses your skin and selects an appropriate protocol — safety is always confirmed before treatment.
It depends on your concern. Pigmentation and toning are usually treated over a series of sessions spaced a few weeks apart, while some texture goals need fewer. Your physician will outline a realistic plan at consultation.
Laser toning and pico treatments typically have little downtime, while resurfacing may involve 3–7 days of recovery with some redness or flaking. Your physician will advise you fully beforehand.
