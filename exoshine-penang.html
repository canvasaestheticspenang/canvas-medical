What is Exoshine?

Exosomes are nano-sized vesicles that cells use to send regenerative instructions to one another — concentrated packets of growth factors, proteins and signalling molecules. Applied to skin prepared by microneedling or energy treatments, exosome preparations like Exoshine deliver those renewal signals directly where ageing and damage have slowed the skin's own messaging.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Exoshine is used two ways: as a regenerative booster in its own right — glow, texture, fine-line softening over a short course — and as a recovery accelerant layered onto laser, RF or microneedling sessions, where patients typically notice calmer, faster-settling skin.

How it works

  1. Consultation & plan. Your physician decides whether Exoshine runs as its own course or layered onto your energy-based programme.
  2. Preparation. Microneedling — or your scheduled laser/RF session — creates the delivery pathway.
  3. Exosome application. Exoshine is applied across the prepared skin, delivering regenerative signals into the microchannels.
  4. Course & review. Two to four sessions complete a typical course; recovery-focused use follows your treatment calendar.

Exosome therapy at CANVAS is a physician-supervised aesthetic treatment; it is not a treatment for medical skin disease. Not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active infection. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

What the evidence honestly says

Exosome skincare is young science: laboratory and early clinical work supports improved healing, collagen activity and post-procedure recovery, and clinical experience is accumulating quickly — but the large randomised trials that anchor older categories are still coming. We say that plainly.

Where exosomes already earn their keep at CANVAS is as an amplifier: layered onto microneedling, laser or RF, where calmer, faster recovery is consistently observed. As standalone renewal, results are promising and best framed as progressive skin-quality gains, not transformation.
