What is Regenerative & Wellness at CANVAS?

Therapeutic plasma exchange (TPE) — also called apheresis or plasmapheresis — is an established medical procedure in which blood is drawn, the plasma fraction is separated and exchanged, and the blood components are returned to the body. In doing so, accumulated waste proteins, inflammatory mediators and other plasma-borne factors are removed. Long used in hospital medicine, TPE is now also applied in physician-supervised wellness and longevity programmes.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, TPE sits alongside a menu of physician-formulated intravenous infusion therapies — nutrient, hydration and recovery protocols matched to your screening results and goals. Everything in this programme is medical: prescribed by a doctor, preceded by screening, and delivered in a monitored clinical setting at Gurney Walk.

How it works

  1. Screening & consultation. Your physician reviews your health history and blood work, and determines whether TPE, infusion therapy — or neither — is appropriate for you.
  2. Protocol design. Session type, exchange volumes or infusion formulation, and programme frequency are prescribed individually, not sold as packages first.
  3. Monitored treatment session. TPE sessions run through a dedicated apheresis system with continuous monitoring; infusions are delivered comfortably over a supervised sitting.
  4. Review & programme. Response and blood parameters are reviewed before any further sessions are scheduled.

TPE is not suitable for everyone — screening covers cardiovascular status, blood parameters and medical history, and our physicians will decline treatment where it is not appropriate. This programme supports wellness and is not a treatment for specific diseases.

TPE vs ordinary IV drips

An IV drip adds — fluids, vitamins, micronutrients. Therapeutic plasma exchange removes: the apheresis system separates your plasma, together with the waste proteins and inflammatory mediators it carries, and exchanges it before returning your blood components. They are complementary tools, not competitors — and neither is casually dispensed here.

At CANVAS, both sit inside a physician-led programme: screening first, prescription second, treatment third. If your goals would be better served by something else entirely, we will say so.
