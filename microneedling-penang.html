What is Microneedling?

Microneedling, also called collagen induction therapy, uses fine needles to create controlled micro-channels in the skin. This prompts the skin's natural repair response, supporting smoother texture and renewed firmness. At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, microneedling is performed by trained clinicians under physician supervision.

We offer traditional microneedling and advanced EXION microneedling RF, which combines micro-channelling with radiofrequency energy for deeper remodelling — and can be paired with serums or PRP for added renewal.

EXION Microneedling RF Collagen Induction Acne Scars Skin Texture Pore Refinement With PRP

How it works

  1. Consultation. A CANVAS clinician assesses your skin concern — texture, scarring or pores — and selects the right microneedling approach.
  2. Preparation. The skin is cleansed and numbing cream is applied for comfort.
  3. Treatment. Fine needles create controlled micro-channels; RF energy is added with EXION for deeper remodelling where indicated.
  4. Recovery & renewal. The skin repairs over the following days; your physician plans a series for progressive results.

FAQ

Microneedling, answered.

Microneedling is commonly used for skin texture, acne scarring, fine lines and enlarged-looking pores. Your clinician will confirm whether it suits your concern at consultation.
A series is usually recommended — often a few sessions spaced several weeks apart — as collagen renewal builds gradually. Your physician will plan this with you.
Numbing cream is applied beforehand, so most patients are comfortable. You may feel light pressure or vibration; RF microneedling adds a warming sensation.
You can expect mild redness, similar to light sunburn, for a day or two. RF microneedling may extend this slightly. Your physician will guide aftercare and sun protection.
