What is Lanluma?

Lanluma is a poly-L-lactic acid (PLLA) collagen stimulator formulated in larger-volume vials specifically so it can treat the body as well as the face. Like all PLLA, it does not fill directly: it seeds the tissue with microparticles that trigger your own collagen production, rebuilding volume gradually and naturally.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Lanluma extends collagen biostimulation beyond the face — smoothing hip dips, adding shape to the buttocks, and treating crepey volume loss on the arms, thighs and décolletage — without implants or surgery. On the face, it behaves as a classic gradual PLLA rejuvenation.

How it works

  1. Consultation & planning. Your physician assesses the treatment area — facial deflation or body contour — and maps realistic vial counts and sessions.
  2. Treatment session. Reconstituted Lanluma is distributed across the planned zone. Sessions are comfortable, with numbing available where needed.
  3. Collagen builds. Initial water volume settles within days; your own collagen then develops progressively over two to three months.
  4. Course & review. Two to three sessions several weeks apart are typical, with shape reviewed and refined as it develops.

Who it's for

Lanluma may be suitable if you:

  • Want to smooth hip dips or add buttock shape without implants
  • Have volume loss or crepey skin on the arms, thighs or décolletage
  • Prefer gradual, natural change built from your own collagen
  • Are looking for facial PLLA rejuvenation with a body option available
  • Are comfortable with a course of sessions rather than a single visit
  • Want physician-led planning around realistic, anatomical goals

Lanluma is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or with active infection in the treatment area. Body contouring results depend on individual collagen response and are reviewed across the course. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Lanluma vs Sculptra

Both are poly-L-lactic acid collagen stimulators, and on the face they behave similarly. The practical difference is formulation and intent: Lanluma's larger-volume vials are designed for body work — hips, buttocks, thighs, arms — where meaningful contouring requires more product, sensibly dosed.

At CANVAS both are on formulary. For facial-only plans, your physician may recommend either; for body contouring, Lanluma is generally the tool designed for the job. See also Sculptra in Penang.
