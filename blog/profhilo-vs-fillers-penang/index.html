Profhilo vs Dermal Fillers:
Which Is Right for You?

Both are injectable treatments. Both use hyaluronic acid. But Profhilo and dermal fillers do very different things — and mixing them up is one of the most common misunderstandings we see at CANVAS.

The short answer

Profhilo is a skin-quality treatment. Fillers are a volume and structure treatment. They address different problems, and in many cases our physicians use both — just in different areas, for different reasons.

If you come to CANVAS wondering which you need, the answer almost always starts with: what is bothering you most?

What is Profhilo?

Profhilo is an injectable containing a very high concentration of pure hyaluronic acid — but it is not a filler. Instead of adding volume to a specific area, Profhilo spreads through the tissue, deeply hydrating the skin and stimulating collagen and elastin production.

The result, after two sessions spaced a month apart, is skin that looks rehydrated, smoother and more elastic. Patients often describe it as their skin looking rested and healthy — without looking done.

Skin quality Hydration Collagen stimulation Laxity improvement Natural look

What are Dermal Fillers?

Dermal fillers are injectable gels — most commonly hyaluronic acid — used to restore or add volume to specific areas. At CANVAS, our physicians use fillers for purposes such as:

  • Restoring volume loss in the cheeks or temples
  • Softening the appearance of deep nasolabial folds
  • Defining the jawline or chin
  • Subtle lip enhancement
  • Under-eye hollowness (tear trough)

The right filler for the right area matters enormously. We carry a wide range — from thin skinbooster-type products to denser structural fillers — because no single product suits every purpose or face.

Can you combine them?

Yes — and often the best outcomes come from combining both. A common approach at CANVAS is to use a structural filler in the cheeks or chin to restore lost volume, and Profhilo across the face to improve skin quality. You get the architecture of a well-rested face and the skin quality to match.

Your physician will assess your face holistically and recommend what genuinely makes sense — not what produces the highest invoice. Our philosophy is always less-is-more.

How to decide

You may be better suited to Profhilo if you:

  • Feel your skin has lost its healthy, hydrated glow
  • Notice early laxity or dullness but don't have significant volume loss
  • Want a natural result with minimal visible change
  • Are newer to injectables and looking for a gentle starting point

You may be better suited to fillers if you:

  • Have noticeable volume loss in the cheeks, temples or under-eyes
  • Want to define or balance facial contours
  • Have deep folds or hollows that hydration alone won't address
  • Are looking for a specific structural change in a particular area
