What is Pigmentation Treatment?

Pigmentation is not one condition. Superficial sun spots and freckles sit near the surface and clear relatively readily; melasma is a deeper, hormone- and sun-influenced condition that relapses and can be worsened by the wrong laser; post-inflammatory pigmentation follows acne or injury. Treating them identically is why so much pigmentation returns — often darker.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, pigmentation is handled as medicine: diagnosis first, then a combined, staged plan — the right laser at the right settings, brightening boosters, prescribed topicals and non-negotiable sun protection. In a tropical, high-UV city, we are candid that maintenance and daily SPF are part of the treatment, not optional extras.

How it works

  1. Diagnosis. Your physician identifies the pigment type — sun spots, melasma, post-inflammatory — because each is treated differently.
  2. Staged plan. A combined approach is sequenced: appropriate laser, brightening boosters, prescribed topicals and sun protection.
  3. Careful treatment. Pigment is treated conservatively — especially melasma — to clear it without provoking relapse.
  4. Maintenance & review. Results are held with maintenance and rigorous SPF, reviewed over time.

Who it's for

Pigmentation Treatment may be suitable if you:

  • Have sun spots, freckles or age spots you want cleared
  • Have melasma and want it managed safely, without making it worse
  • Have dark marks left behind by acne or injury
  • Have uneven overall tone despite good skincare
  • Want a diagnosis-led plan, not a one-off laser
  • Accept that maintenance and sun protection are part of it

Melasma is a chronic, relapsing condition that is managed rather than cured, and can be aggravated by aggressive treatment. Any changing or unusual pigmented lesion must be assessed by a physician to exclude anything requiring different management. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Why diagnosis comes before the laser

The single most important step in pigmentation treatment is also the one most often skipped: deciding what the pigment actually is. Sun spots want one approach; melasma wants a far more conservative one; post-inflammatory pigment wants patience and the right topicals. A laser that clears sun spots can worsen melasma.

That is why CANVAS treats pigmentation as physician-led medicine, combining pico laser, brightening boosters and prescribed care — and why we are honest that some pigmentation, melasma especially, is a long-term management relationship.
