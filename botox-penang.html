What is Botox?

Botox is the most recognised brand name for botulinum toxin type A — a purified protein that temporarily relaxes specific muscles when injected in small, precise amounts. By reducing targeted muscle activity, it softens the overlying skin and reduces the appearance of dynamic wrinkles caused by repetitive facial expressions.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, every botulinum toxin treatment is planned and administered by an LCP-certified physician. We assess your facial anatomy individually and dose precisely — so you leave looking refreshed and natural, not treated.

How it works

  1. Consultation & assessment. Your physician examines your facial muscles and expressions at rest and in motion. We discuss your goals and explain realistic outcomes — including what Botox cannot address, and whether it should be combined with other treatments.
  2. Marking & dosing. Injection points are mapped to your individual anatomy. Dosing is planned precisely — we avoid over-treatment as a principle.
  3. Injection. Very fine needles deliver small amounts of botulinum toxin to the targeted muscles. Most patients describe minimal discomfort; the procedure takes 15–20 minutes.
  4. Review at 2 weeks. Results develop over 3–7 days and are fully visible at 2 weeks. We offer a review appointment to assess the outcome and make any minor adjustments if needed.

Benefits

Dynamic wrinkle reduction

Softens forehead lines, frown lines and crow's feet caused by repetitive muscle movement.

Non-surgical brow lift

Precise placement can elevate the brow arch and open the eye area without surgery.

Jaw slimming

Masseter Botox reduces the bulk of the jaw muscle over a series of treatments, refining the lower face.

Preventative ageing

Starting Botox before lines become static can slow the formation of permanent creases over time.

Quick, minimal downtime

Most patients return to their day immediately. Avoid strenuous exercise for 4 hours post-treatment.

Reversible & temporary

Effects are temporary (3–5 months), so there is no permanent commitment — results adjust with each session.

Who it's for

Botox may be appropriate if you:

  • Have fine lines or wrinkles that appear or worsen with facial expressions
  • Want to soften a heavy brow or open the eye area without surgery
  • Are looking to slim or soften a square jaw caused by masseter hypertrophy
  • Struggle with excessive sweating (hyperhidrosis) under the arms, hands or scalp
  • Want to begin a preventative programme before lines become static and deeper
  • Are in good general health with realistic expectations

Botox is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or if you have certain neuromuscular conditions. All contraindications are assessed at consultation.
