What is Pico Laser?

A pico (picosecond) laser fires energy in pulses a thousand times shorter than older nanosecond lasers. Because the pulses are so brief, they shatter pigment through a photomechanical shockwave — breaking it into finer particles with less surrounding heat. Finer particles clear more easily, and less heat means gentler treatment for pigment-prone Asian skin.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, our PicoStar platform treats sun spots, freckles, melasma (with care), uneven tone and dullness, plus tattoo removal — settings tuned by the physician to each concern. For a region where sun-driven pigmentation is near-universal, a pico laser is one of the most versatile tools in the laser range.

How it works

  1. Consultation & assessment. Your physician diagnoses the pigment type — crucial, as melasma and sun spots need different handling — and sets the plan.
  2. Tailored laser session. Pico pulses are delivered at settings matched to your concern and skin type, protecting surrounding skin.
  3. Clearance. Shattered pigment is cleared by the body over the following days to weeks; tone evens progressively.
  4. Course & review. Pigment and tone build over a course, reviewed and adjusted to your response.

Who it's for

Pico Laser may be suitable if you:

  • Have sun spots, freckles or uneven pigmentation
  • Want brighter, more even overall tone and glow
  • Have melasma and want it managed carefully by a physician
  • Are seeking tattoo removal
  • Have pigment-prone skin and want a gentler laser approach
  • Are building a pigment and tone programme

Melasma is a chronic, relapsing condition that requires careful, conservative laser handling and a broader plan; aggressive treatment can worsen it. Pico laser is not suitable over active infection or during pregnancy; your physician will diagnose and advise honestly. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Pico laser vs Dermablate resurfacing

Different lasers for different jobs. Pico laser targets pigment and tone with ultra-short pulses and minimal downtime. Dermablate is an Er:YAG resurfacing laser that removes surface layers to renew texture, fine lines and scars, with more recovery.

Pigment and toning: pico. Texture and resurfacing: Dermablate. Many programmes use both, and for stubborn pigment your physician may combine laser with a full pigmentation plan.
