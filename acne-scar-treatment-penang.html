What is Acne Scar Treatment?

Atrophic acne scars come in types — rolling (broad, shallow depressions), boxcar (sharp-edged), ice-pick (narrow and deep) — often mixed on the same face, and frequently accompanied by post-inflammatory pigmentation. Each type responds best to a different tool, which is why single-treatment approaches so often disappoint: no one device addresses every scar.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, acne scarring is treated as the multi-tool problem it is. After classifying your scars, our physicians combine and stage the right treatments — EXION Microneedling RF and laser resurfacing to remodel, subcision to release tethered scars, and Rejuran or other boosters to support healing — over a realistic course.

How it works

  1. Scar classification. Your physician identifies your scar types — rolling, boxcar, ice-pick, mixed — and any pigmentation, because each needs a different tool.
  2. Staged combination plan. The right treatments are sequenced: RF microneedling, laser, subcision and boosters, matched to your scars.
  3. Treatment course. Modalities are delivered over a course, each with its own short recovery, remodelling the skin progressively.
  4. Review & refine. Progress is reviewed over months and the plan refined as scars improve.

Why one treatment rarely fixes acne scars

The most common reason acne-scar treatment disappoints is a single tool used on mixed scars. Resurfacing smooths sharp-edged boxcar scars but won't lift a tethered rolling scar; subcision releases rolling scars but won't resurface texture; ice-pick scars often need focal treatment of their own. Real improvement comes from combining tools, matched to scar type and staged over time.

That is exactly how CANVAS approaches it — RF microneedling, laser, subcision and regenerative boosters in a physician-led plan — and why we are honest that scars are improved substantially rather than erased.
