What is Linerase?

Linerase is an injectable of purified heterologous type I collagen — the structural protein that gives young skin its thickness and spring, and the one that declines steadily from our thirties. Rather than asking the skin to make collagen (as biostimulators do), Linerase supplies it directly to the dermis while also stimulating your fibroblasts to contribute their own.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Linerase is chosen for skin that has begun to thin, crease and lose resilience — often on the mid and lower face and neck — and for patients who want restructuring rather than volume. It layers naturally with HA boosters, which handle hydration while Linerase handles architecture.

How it works

  1. Consultation & screening. Your physician assesses dermal quality and screens suitability for a collagen-based product.
  2. Treatment session. Reconstituted collagen is injected evenly through the dermis of the treatment area, with numbing available.
  3. Restructuring — weeks 1 to 3. Supplied collagen integrates while fibroblast activity adds your own; skin gains thickness and spring.
  4. Course & maintenance. Three to four sessions two to three weeks apart complete a course; maintenance is typically every six to nine months.

Who it's for

Linerase may be suitable if you:

  • Notice thinning, creasing or slackness in facial or neck skin
  • Want the skin itself improved rather than volumised
  • Have early marionette or nasolabial creasing from dermal weakness
  • Prefer course-based regenerative work with durable maintenance
  • Are layering treatments — boosters for hydration, collagen for structure
  • Have realistic, quality-focused goals

Linerase involves animal-derived collagen; allergy screening applies. It is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active skin infection. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Linerase vs collagen biostimulators

Biostimulators like Sculptra or Gouri persuade your fibroblasts to build collagen over months. Linerase shortcuts the first step by delivering type I collagen directly into the dermis — with fibroblast stimulation as the second act rather than the whole play.

Practically: biostimulators excel where volume is part of the goal; Linerase excels where the skin sheet itself — thin, creased, inelastic — is the problem. Many CANVAS plans sequence both.
