What is Chemical Peels?

Chemical peels renew skin through controlled exfoliation: an acid solution loosens and removes damaged surface cells, prompting fresher, more even skin to replace them and stimulating renewal beneath. The art is in depth and formula — glycolic for glow and texture, salicylic for oily and acne-prone skin, mandelic for sensitive or pigment-prone skin, TCA for deeper renewal — each matched to the person.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, our peel range spans the respected NeoStrata and MCCM systems, physician-selected so the acid, strength and depth suit your skin and concern rather than a one-peel-fits-all. For pigment-prone Asian skin especially, choosing the right peel — and not over-doing it — is what separates a glow from a problem.

How it works

  1. Consultation & skin analysis. Your physician assesses your skin type and concern and selects the acid, strength and depth to suit.
  2. Peel application. The solution is applied and neutralised to a controlled depth; a tingle or warmth is normal.
  3. Renewal. Superficial peels glow immediately; medium peels flake over days as fresher skin emerges.
  4. Course & maintenance. Peels are often staged in a course, with sun protection essential throughout.

Who it's for

Chemical Peels may be suitable if you:

  • Have dull, uneven or rough-textured skin
  • Have oily, congested or acne-prone skin
  • Want a glow with little or no downtime
  • Have mild pigmentation or want more even tone
  • Prefer a peel depth chosen by a physician for your skin
  • Are building a regular skin-renewal routine

Peel depth and formula must suit your skin type — the wrong peel can aggravate pigment-prone skin. Peels are not suitable over active infection, on broken skin, or during pregnancy for certain agents. A physician confirms suitability and sun protection is essential. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Chemical peels vs PRX-T33

Both renew the skin, differently. A traditional chemical peel exfoliates to a controlled depth — the skin renews, with glow (superficial) or some peeling and downtime (medium). PRX-T33 uses TCA but protects the surface, giving bio-stimulation and firmness with no shedding.

Genuine exfoliation and renewal: a peel. Firmness and glow with no downtime: PRX-T33. Your CANVAS physician will choose — and often both have a place across the year.
