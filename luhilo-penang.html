What is Luhilo?

Luhilo is a Korean skinbooster whose selling point is concentration: a dense hyaluronic acid payload per session, formulated to saturate skin that ordinary hydration cannot reach. HA binds many times its weight in water — delivered at this concentration, the effect is the plump, cushioned, light-catching finish Korean aesthetics turned into a global standard.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, Luhilo is the K-beauty entry in our booster formulary — chosen for patients whose skin runs chronically dry or dull and who want the fastest route to visible dewiness, on a maintenance rhythm that keeps it.

High-concentration HA K-beauty dewiness Deep hydration Plump, cushioned finish Face & neck Course + maintenance rhythm

How it works

  1. Consultation & skin review. Your physician confirms dehydration and dullness are the target — and Luhilo the right concentration for your skin.
  2. Treatment session. High-concentration HA is delivered evenly across the face; numbing keeps it comfortable.
  3. Saturation builds. Hydration compounds session on session; glow follows water.
  4. Course & maintenance. Two to three sessions, then top-ups every three to six months.

Who it's for

Luhilo may be suitable if you:

  • Have chronically dry, dull or tight-feeling skin
  • Chase the plump K-beauty 'water glow' finish
  • Find standard boosters fade too fast on your skin
  • Wear makeup that clings to dry patches
  • Want hydration without any volume or shape change
  • Like predictable course-plus-maintenance treatments

Luhilo is not suitable during pregnancy or breastfeeding, or over active skin infection or acne flares. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Luhilo vs Luhilo Snow

Same house, different briefs. Luhilo is the hydration flagship — maximum HA payload for plumpness and dew. Luhilo Snow reformulates around brightening, pairing HA with a complex aimed at tone and radiance for skin that is dull in colour, not just dry.

Thirsty skin: Luhilo. Sallow or uneven-toned skin: Snow. Both: your CANVAS physician will sequence them — hydration first is the usual order.
