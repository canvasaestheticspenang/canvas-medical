What is Laser Hair Removal?

Laser hair removal works by selective photothermolysis: the laser's energy is absorbed by melanin (pigment) in the hair shaft and converted to heat that disables the follicle. Because follicles cycle through growth and rest phases and the laser only affects those actively growing, a course of sessions spaced weeks apart is essential to progressively reduce hair — no single session removes it all.

At CANVAS Medical Clinic in George Town, Penang, laser hair removal is delivered under physician supervision with settings matched to your skin tone and hair type — important for both safety and results, particularly across the range of skin tones we treat. Common areas include underarms, legs, arms, bikini, face, back and chest.

How it works

  1. Consultation & patch guidance. Your physician assesses skin tone and hair type and sets safe, effective parameters for the area.
  2. Treatment session. Laser energy is delivered across the area, disabling actively-growing follicles; sessions are quick for small areas.
  3. Shedding. Treated hairs shed over one to two weeks; the area grows back progressively sparser.
  4. Course & maintenance. Sessions several weeks apart catch follicles across their cycles; occasional maintenance sustains the result.

  • Want underarms, legs, bikini, back or facial hair treated

Laser hair removal targets pigment in the hair, so it works best on dark hair and is less effective on very light, grey or red hair. Settings must be matched to skin tone for safety; it is not performed over active infection or during pregnancy. A course is required — no single session is permanent. Suitability is always confirmed at consultation.

Why laser hair removal takes a course

Hair grows in cycles — only follicles in their active growth phase absorb enough laser energy to be disabled, and at any moment only a fraction are in that phase. That is the honest reason no single session clears everything, and why treatments are spaced weeks apart: each catches a different set of follicles as they cycle into growth.

Expect a course of several sessions for meaningful, lasting reduction, with occasional maintenance afterwards. Your CANVAS physician will estimate the number for your area and hair type — and be straight that 'permanent' means long-lasting reduction, not a guarantee of zero regrowth forever.
